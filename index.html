A VAPE OR ELECTRONIC CIGARETTE IS A DEVICE THAT HEATS A LIQUID (OFTEN CALLED E-LIQUID OR VAPE JUICE) INTO AN AEROSOL THAT IS INHALED BY THE USER. VAPES COME IN A VARIETY OF SHAPES AND SIZES, FROM SMALL AND PORTABLE DEVICES TO LARGER, MORE POWERFUL MODS. THEY CONSIST OF A BATTERY, A TANK OR CARTRIDGE TO HOLD THE E-LIQUID, AND A HEATING ELEMENT OR COIL THAT VAPORIZES THE LIQUID.

VAPES CAN BE USED WITH A VARIETY OF E-LIQUIDS, WHICH COME IN DIFFERENT FLAVORS AND NICOTINE STRENGTHS. SOME E- LIQUIDS ARE NICOTINE-FREE, WHILE OTHERS CONTAIN VARYING LEVELS OF NICOTINE. VAPING IS OFTEN SEEN AS AN ALTERNATIVE TO SMOKING TRADITIONAL CIGARETTES, AS IT ALLOWS USERS TO GET THEIR NICOTINE FIX WITHOUT THE HARMFUL EFFECTS OF SMOKING TOBACCO.

WHILE VAPING IS GENERALLY CONSIDERED TO BE LESS HARMFUL THAN SMOKING, IT IS NOT WITHOUT RISKS. THE LONG-TERM HEALTH EFFECTS OF VAPING ARE STILL NOT FULLY UNDERSTOOD, AND THERE HAVE BEEN REPORTS OF SERIOUS LUNG DAMAGE AND OTHER HEALTH PROBLEMS RELATED TO VAPING. OVERALL, VAPING IS A POPULAR AND RAPIDLY GROWING INDUSTRY, WITH MILLIONS OF PEOPLE AROUND THE WORLD USING VAPES AS A SMOKING ALTERNATIVE OR SIMPLY FOR THE ENJOYMENT OF THE FLAVORS AND SENSATIONS THEY PROVIDE.
